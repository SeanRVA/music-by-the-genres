Level 4: Country Music

What is Country Music?

Country music, or western music, is a genre of music originating from the Southern and Southwestern regions of the United States. This genre takes inspiration from a lot of other genres of music, like cowboy Western styles, American folk music, blues, and others. It gained traction and became more popular in the 1920s. Since then, country has expanded and is now an umbrella term for a variety of sub-genres.

Country music often has simple forms, folk lyrics, and extensively used blues modes. The music is often used for dance or as a ballad.

Country music instrumentation often includes instruments like banjos, fiddles, harmonicas, acoustic and steel guitars, and others. Newer, modernized forms of country may also use drums and different modern instruments, but these are a center of controversy since some country music-lovers think they aren't "pure". Vocals, like in pop and rock, are very important as they drive the message of the lyrics home.

Characteristics of Country Music

  • Themes are usually on family, rural life, love, and the like
  • Have sense of intimacy since they are usually written by rural folk
  • Vocal quality often has "twang", mostly harmonizing at the chorus
  • Usually soothing and calm
  • Not often syncopated, usually written in common time(4/4)
